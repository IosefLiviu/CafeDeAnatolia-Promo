**Format:** 9:16 (Vertical) + 16:9 (Horizontal — priority for Facebook/YouTube Ads)

## CINEMATOGRAPHY NOTES

| Aspect | Specification |
|--------|---------------|
| Frame rate | Shoot at 60fps or 120fps, play back at 24fps for smooth slow-motion |
| Lens | 50mm or 85mm for close-ups (shallow DOF). 24mm for wide crowd/venue shots |
| Lighting | Practical lights only — candles, warm LEDs, tungsten. No harsh/cold light |
| Smoke/haze | Essential for every shot. Use haze machine, not heavy fog |
| Camera movement | Minimal. Slow dollies, slow push-ins. Nothing handheld or shaky |
| Aspect ratio | Optional: 2.39:1 letterbox for extra cinematic feel (black bars top/bottom) |

## COLOR GRADING

- Shadows: pushed toward deep brown/chocolate (not pure black)
- Highlights: warm amber/gold
- Midtones: slightly desaturated, skin tones warm
- Film grain: subtle, organic (not digital noise)
- Reference: search "Tulum party cinematography" or "Cafe De Anatolia official videos" for tone

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- This is the hero video — spend the most time here
- Every shot should feel like a photograph. Composition matters.
- No fast cuts. Longest average shot duration of all 5 scripts.
- If shooting at Grand Hotel Bucharest, scout the venue first — find the best angles, the most photogenic corridors/rooms
- If using stock footage, Artgrid has the best cinematic club/music footage. Search: "intimate party", "DJ close-up cinematic", "dancing silhouettes"
- The transition from VO to music-only at 0:17 should feel like a "release" — the audience exhales
